About the Symposium

The WTD Symposium is an annual two-day convening that blends science, knowledge translation, dialogue, and collaboration through panel discussions, roundtables, and interactive workshops. Beyond its program, it offers meaningful networking moments that spark authentic exchange and lasting partnerships.

Bringing together researchers, clinicians, policymakers, people living with dementia, care partners, patient associations, care providers, NGOs, advocates, artists, and innovators, the Symposium cultivates a global community dedicated to advancing innovation, leadership, and real-world impact in dementia research, care, and policy.

Guiding Principles

Each edition of the Symposium is grounded in principles that celebrate diversity, inclusion, and collaboration, turning ideas into action and experience into evidence.

Patient & Public Involvement

Lived and professional expertise are equally valued, driving co-created innovation and shared ownership of outcomes.

Framing

Priorities are identified in dementia care, equity, and leadership through diverse academic and community lenses.

Diversity

Perspectives are connected across disciplines, cultures, and experiences to spark new collaborations.

Action-Focused

Ideas are transformed into real-world strategies that influence policy, practice, and public awareness.

Why Does It Matter?

Inclusive science leads to better science!

The Symposium is part of a growing movement recognizing that innovation in dementia cannot happen without the active participation of those most affected.

By bridging academic research, lived experience, and policy, WTD builds a more equitable and human-centered approach to dementia research and care.
